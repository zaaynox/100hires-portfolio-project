## Post 1
**Topic:** Brand marketing beats AEO checklists for LLM visibility

There are tactical steps to improve a site or brand's visibility in LLMs, but most of what I see companies doing in practice fits this meme. If a company already has high search visibility due to a strong brand and effective SEO, it will likely have high visibility in LLMs with little additional effort. Based on my experience with many brands, the best way to improve LLM visibility is through brand marketing, NOT the AEO checklist sold by AEO agencies, AI-generated content, or Reddit astroturfing citation-building campaigns that many companies are investing in.

## Post 2
**Topic:** The real cost of AI content at scale and what recovery actually looks like

Some SEO teams genuinely thought they'd cracked the code. AI-generated content at scale pumping out thousands of pages. Programmatic everything. They called it a strategy, but it was closer to machine translation with a keyword list. Glenn Gabe calls this Mt AI, and I love this term. Google's updates didn't just slow them down. It wiped out years of work in a few algorithm cycles. Traffic gone. The smug case studies quietly deleted. The question they are asking is: how do we recover? Wrong question. You can't recover by doing more of the same thing that got you penalized. Publishing "better" AI content isn't a pivot. It's just slower self-destruction. The actual pivot looks like this: Stop asking what keywords you can rank for. Start asking what problems your users actually have. Build content around intent, not search volume. Write things people would share even if Google didn't exist. Don't try to outsmart the algorithm. Focus on being genuinely useful to real people, and the traffic will follow. Nobody wanted to hear this when the content farm was printing traffic. Finally, they're hearing it now.

## Post 3
**Topic:** AI search panic is giving SEO a seat at the table it never had

The AI search panic might be the best thing to ever happen to SEO. Not because AEO is some magic new discipline, it's not new. But because, for the first time in years, the C-suite is actually asking about organic. Performance marketing got the budget. Paid got the headcount. SEO got a Notion doc and a prayer. Organic has always been the channel that quietly drove outsized returns while being chronically underfunded. Now, suddenly, because LLMs exist and people are worried about traffic, everyone wants to talk about it. Executives are asking how they show up in AI answers. Boards want to understand search visibility. Budget conversations that used to stall are moving. It's fine with me that some of the AEO discourse is overblown because the noise gets SEO a seat at the table, which will translate into real headcount and a budget that reflects the channel's actual value.

## Post 4
**Topic:** AEO is not SEO 2.0 and treating it that way misses the point entirely

AEO is not SEO 2.0. And everyone treating it like an upgrade has already missed the point. SEO was always about a funnel that began with search traffic, so technical precision and link volume were key parts of the playbook. LLMs don't have rankings, don't have a page one, and the click stopped being the KPI a while ago. Most people still haven't adjusted their thinking to match that reality. The part everyone skips over is that you can't manufacture visibility anymore. A scrappy site with the right backlinks could outrank a household name in SEO. That arbitrage is completely dead now. LLMs don't respond to domain authority; they respond to whether people actually know who you are, whether your brand gets referenced in the real world. Citations are not backlinks. You don't acquire your way into an LLM answer by building links or publishing more content. You get there by being a brand that the world already talked about before you started optimizing anything.

## Post 5
**Topic:** As vibe coding lowers the barrier to building, marketing judgment becomes the real bottleneck

Anyone can build almost anything now. Vibe coding has made it possible to spin up real solutions in hours. The problem it creates is that if everyone can build, the bottleneck shifts entirely to getting noticed. AI can handle some of this. There are tools that will autonomously implement SEO best practices, set up and launch paid campaigns, and A/B test copy. That's real, useful automation which removes grunt work. What it can't do is figure out why anyone should care about your product in the first place. It can't find the unexpected angle that makes something click. It can't build the kind of trust that turns curious visitors into loyal users. Driving real adoption is still a creative problem because it requires understanding people, not just patterns. The pool of marketers who can solve these problems isn't growing at the same rate as the apps that will need them.

## Post 6
**Topic:** YouTube is the most underrated lever for AI search visibility, not Reddit

Everyone's chasing Reddit for AI visibility. But there's a bigger opportunity most are ignoring. YouTube. Google is still the dominant search engine, and AI Overviews and AI Mode are how most people will experience LLM results going forward. Google is going to pull from its own ecosystem when building those responses, and YouTube is the crown jewel of that ecosystem. Reddit gets attention because it's text-heavy and LLMs are clearly trained on it. But AI Overviews aren't just about training data, it's about what Google trusts and surfaces right now. YouTube content is indexed by Google directly, tied to authority signals Google already measures, transcribed and readable by AI systems, and preferred in queries with how-to or explainer intent. If you want your brand showing up in Google's AI responses, YouTube is one of the most underrated levers you have. Stop spamming Reddit and focus on building a YouTube audience. YouTube takes more effort, which is exactly why the opportunity is still wide open.
