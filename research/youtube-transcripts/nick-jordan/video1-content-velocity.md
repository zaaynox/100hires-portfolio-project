# Nick Jordan — Video 1: How to Grow Sites to 100k Organic Views Without Link Building

**Source:** https://youtu.be/wW_t3btaxAk
**Channel:** Niche Pursuits Podcast
**Topic:** Content velocity, topical authority, and why publishing volume beats link building for organic growth

---

## Key Insights from Transcript

### Content Velocity Is the Primary Driver of Topical Authority
Nick's core thesis is that the fastest path to ranking is publishing high quality content at high volume. His agency took four projects from zero to 100k organic visitors per month without building a single backlink. The largest grew from zero to 1.5 million organics per month in 24 months, taking a client from seed stage to a 210 million dollar Series B valuation. The mechanism is simple: you cannot rank for a keyword until you have a page about it, so publishing faster starts the rankings clock sooner.

### A 30-Page Website Cannot Be Authoritative
Nick references a statement from Google's John Mueller that it is hard to call a 30-page website authoritative. The logic is that building a 30-page website costs nothing, so Google cannot use it as a trust signal. Publishing 1000 pages, however, requires significant investment and implies an actual business with customers to serve. Content volume acts as a proxy for legitimacy and commitment in Google's eyes.

### Internal Links Do What Most People Think Backlinks Do
When Nick wants to rank a specific page and organic growth has stalled, his first and usually only additional step is to drive as many internal links to that page as possible. He has never needed a second step after that. Internal links signal to Google how important a page is to the brand, and a page with 1000 internal links pointing to it will outperform a page on a larger site with only one internal link, according to his testing.

### Keyword Grouping Is the Foundation of Content at Scale
At high publishing volumes, keyword research becomes a bottleneck. Nick uses keyword clustering tools that use Google's own data to determine which keywords belong on the same page and which need separate pages. The counterintuitive finding is that keywords that seem related often need separate pages, while some that seem different should be combined. If no page currently ranks for two keywords simultaneously, you cannot rank for both on one page either.

### User Engagement Metrics Drive Rankings More Than Technical Factors
Nick's theory of how Google works is that user engagement signals including time on site, pages visited, bounce rate, and return visits are heavily weighted in the ranking algorithm. His evidence is outranking Instagram for Instagram support and Shopify for Shopify-related queries simply by creating more comprehensive, better structured content than the official pages. The official pages had weak URLs, minimal content, and poor structure. His pages matched the full search intent and won.

### The Strategy Works at Any Scale
800 articles per month is the ceiling Nick's agency has reached for one client. But he also has projects doing 20 to 100 articles per month that hit 100k organics. The principle scales down. Whatever volume you can sustain, more is better than less, done faster is better than done slower, and topical depth in a specific subtopic beats scattered coverage across a broad niche.

### AI Content Was Not Ready But the Direction Was Clear
Recorded before the current AI content wave, Nick predicted AI-generated content would arrive sooner than expected after seeing OpenAI's DALL-E image generation. His observation that certain content formats like templated informational pages are perfect candidates for AI automation turned out to be accurate. This video represents the pre-AI baseline that current AI content production strategies are built on top of.

## Transcript Summary

Nick Jordan is the founder of Workello, a content operations company. His approach to SEO is deliberately narrow: he does not do link building or technical SEO, focusing entirely on content volume and topical relevance.

His framework emerged from discovering that he could outrank major brands like Instagram and Shopify for their own support queries by creating more comprehensive content optimized for user intent. This led to a theory that Google weighs user engagement signals heavily, and that the best way to win those signals is to create content that genuinely answers the full scope of what a searcher wants.

At scale, his agency uses keyword clustering tools to group thousands of keywords into individual page targets, then publishes content at the highest velocity the client budget allows. Internal linking is used strategically to concentrate authority toward the most commercially valuable pages. The result across multiple clients is topical authority that produces organic growth without any external link building.

The relevance to AI content production is significant. Nick's content velocity model is the foundation that AI writing tools were built to accelerate. His framework for keyword clustering, topical depth, and internal linking structure translates directly into how AI-powered content operations are run today.
