# Kyle Roof — Video 2: The New Rules of SEO in an AI-Driven World

**Source:** https://youtu.be/lsXdC5fNoW0
**Channel:** The Future Podcast with Chris Brandt
**Topic:** How AI Overviews work, why traditional SEO still matters, EEAT, and competing against big brands

---

## Key Insights from Transcript

### AI Overviews Are Just Glorified Featured Snippets
Kyle's take on AI Overviews is that they are not a fundamentally new thing. They are an expansion of the featured snippet concept using a slightly different method to find answers. Critically, the content powering AI Overviews still comes from the top organic results. To appear in AI Overviews you still have to rank in the top 10 first. The new stuff requires the old stuff.

### The Core Algorithm Has Not Changed
Despite two massive updates and widespread industry panic, Kyle ran his original 2016 tests again and found they all still work exactly the same. Sites that survived the updates and new sites launched after them are doing fine using the same techniques. The organic ranking algorithm has not changed as much as Google wants people to believe.

### Google Dropped From 96% to 92% Market Share
Kyle puts the Google vs other platforms debate in perspective. A 4% drop in market share sounds significant but going from 96% to 92% does not justify reallocating resources away from Google. For most practical searches, people still end up back on Google to verify information even if they started elsewhere.

### Proving You Are Real Is Now Priority One
With AI flooding the internet with content, Google's primary filter has become whether a site represents a real company. Kyle's checklist for proving realness to Google's bots includes having a physical address, a local phone number matching that address, a current copyright, an about page with real humans linked to LinkedIn, multiple department email addresses, and organization schema in your code. Real companies do not have EEAT issues because they naturally do what real companies do.

### Finding Gaps Big Brands Ignore
Large brands go after two-word high-volume terms and ignore three and four word phrases that are often more conversion-focused. These bottom of funnel terms are where smaller sites can win. Another tactic is optimizing for a competitor's pain points, ranking for searches about their problems and offering your solution as the alternative.

### Content Volume With Strategic Intent
Sites that perform well are consistently gaining keywords and impressions by publishing content with a clear reason for existing. The bottleneck is rarely SEO knowledge. It is the ability to produce content at a regular clip in a systematic way with internal linking that pushes authority to target pages. Content for the sake of content is not the point.

### If You Need Big SEO Edits Something Went Wrong Earlier
When a tool tells you that you need to add 80 terms to a page, that is not an SEO problem. That is a content problem. If you answered the question correctly in the first place the SEO edits should be minor tweaks, not a full rewrite.

## Transcript Summary

AI Overviews are essentially expanded featured snippets. The information in them comes from the top organic results, meaning you still have to rank in the top 10 to be eligible. The core ranking algorithm has remained consistent through all recent updates. Kyle re-ran his 2016 tests and they all work the same.

Google's market share dropped roughly 4 percent but that means going from around 96 to 92 percent, which does not justify shifting resources away from Google SEO. For most searches people still return to Google to verify information found elsewhere.

With AI content flooding the web, proving you are a real company has become the most important ranking factor. Google bots cannot look at a site and intuitively know it is real. They go through a checklist. Real companies pass naturally. Sites trying to hide who they are get penalized.

To compete against large brands, find the three and four word phrases they consider too small to target. These are often high-intent, bottom of funnel terms that convert well. You can also optimize for your competitors' weaknesses, ranking for searches about their problems and positioning yourself as the alternative.

Content strategy should be systematic, not random. Every piece should have a clear reason for existing and should be interlinked to push authority toward target pages. Sites that grow consistently are the ones gaining keywords and impressions at a steady clip over time.
