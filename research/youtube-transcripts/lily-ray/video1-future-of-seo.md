## Key Insights from Transcript

### Google Core Updates Have One Consistent Goal
Across nearly 10 years of analyzing core updates, Lily identifies a consistent pattern. Google is always trying to improve result quality, reduce spam, and demote sites doing too much SEO. When a tactic works well and becomes widely adopted, Google reverse engineers it and penalizes it. The helpful content update was the clearest example of this cycle playing out at scale.

### GEO Spam Is in the Same Early Phase as Pre-Penguin SEO
We are currently in the early days of spammy GEO tactics working before a crackdown. Self-promotional listicles, Reddit spam campaigns, and AI-generated content at scale are all producing short-term results that Lily expects will collapse. She calls it Google preparing to drop an atomic bomb on manipulative AI search tactics, similar to what happened with Penguin and the helpful content update.

### Self-Promotional Listicles Are Already Backfiring
Creating articles that rank your own brand number one for best-in-category queries worked temporarily but models are already detecting it. Claude has begun explicitly flagging spammed categories and telling users it is using third-party sources instead of self-promotional ones. As LLMs develop spam detection similar to what Google built over decades, this tactic will become a liability rather than an asset.

### The Only Sustainable Path Is Real EEAT
The most durable answer to both SEO and GEO visibility is the same. Become a real human or brand that is known in your industry. Build presence across YouTube, LinkedIn, Reddit, and other platforms that search engines and LLMs actively cite. Put out original ideas that demonstrate genuine expertise rather than letting AI do all the thinking. Real companies with real people do not have EEAT issues because they naturally do what earns trust.

### Less Is More in SEO Right Now
Lily's current philosophy is that content bloat is a liability. Google has said 50% of the web they crawl is spam. Sites with large amounts of low-quality content drag down the quality perception of the entire domain. Removing bloat should always come before creating new content. A smaller set of genuinely useful pages outperforms a large site with mediocre content.

### Recovery From Core Updates Takes Longer Than People Expect
Recovery from a major algorithm hit realistically takes 6 months to 2 years, and is getting harder over time. The helpful content update left some sites permanently damaged. The key steps are ruling out technical issues first, then auditing all content against Google's helpful content guidelines honestly, removing problematic content, and demonstrating through new content that the brand deserves to be trusted again.

### AI Mode Will Not Fully Replace Traditional Search Because of Ad Revenue
Google cannot be profitable if AI Mode replaces traditional search results in their current form. The ad revenue model depends on sponsored links that look like organic results appearing above the fold. Until Google can replicate that revenue in AI Mode, the experience will remain a blend of traditional results and AI features rather than a full switch.

### Internal Linking Is the Most Underrated Tactic Nobody Talks About
When asked about tactics that work incredibly well but rarely get discussed, Lily's immediate answer is internal linking. It helps both search engines and users navigate a site simultaneously, costs nothing, and has no spam risk. Combined with conversion rate optimization and strong trust signals like author bios and credentials, it consistently moves the needle without any of the downside risk of more aggressive tactics.

## Transcript Summary

Lily Ray is VP of SEO at Amsive and one of the most recognized voices in technical SEO and algorithm analysis. She has tracked Google core updates for nearly a decade and specializes in helping sites recover from major ranking losses.

Her core message across this conversation is that the patterns playing out in GEO and AI search today are identical to the patterns that preceded Google's biggest crackdowns in traditional SEO. Tactics that work in the short term get widely adopted, create a bad user experience, attract public criticism, and then get aggressively penalized. Self-promotional listicles and Reddit spam campaigns are the current version of this cycle.

The sustainable alternative is building genuine authority through real expertise, original ideas, and presence across the platforms that both Google and LLMs actively crawl and cite. Real brands with real people pass quality filters naturally without needing to engineer around them.

On the technical side, Lily emphasizes that content quality and site health should always be addressed before expansion. Less high-quality content consistently outperforms more mediocre content, and recovery from algorithm penalties is measured in years rather than months.
